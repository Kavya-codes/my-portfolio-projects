Stephen Hawking

A tribute to the brilliant mind of modern cosmology

Timeline of Stephen Hawking's Life:

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England
  • 1959 - Enrolled at University College, Oxford
  • 1963 - Diagnosed with ALS, given two years to live
  • 1966 - Completes PhD in theoretical physics at Cambridge
  • 1970 - Publishes first breakthrough on black holes
  • 1974 - Elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1979 - Becomes Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge
  • 1988 - Publishes "A Brief History of Time"
  • 2009 - Receives Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2018 - Dies peacefully at home in Cambridge

Contributions to Science:

Stephen Hawking's contributions to theoretical physics and cosmology are profound and include:

  • Proposing the theory of Hawking radiation from black holes
  • Developing the concept of the singularity theorem in general relativity
  • Exploring the origin and nature of the universe through his work on the Big Bang theory
  • Advancing understanding of gravitational singularities and the physics of black holes
  • Contributing to the theory of quantum gravity and the intersection of quantum mechanics with general relativity
